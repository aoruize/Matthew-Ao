About Me

Matthew Ao

Toronto, Canada

I'm a 17 year old programmer who's passionate about artificial intelligence, brain-computer interfaces, and their impact on the future of society. On one hand, I'm worried about the acceleration of technological development and the advent of the fourth industrial revolution. Students like me are still learning about subjects that were taught in school half a century ago—meanwhile industry experts can't even agree on what the job market will look like in ten years. On the other hand, I believe there will always be a place for human innovation and empathy in solving the world's greatest challenges. With this in mind, I like to spend my time sharpening my computer skills, reading, and meeting brilliant like-minded people. I am learning as much as I can about exponential technologies, and I'm on a mission to join Neuralink as one of the pioneering figures in brain-computer interface technology. I hope we work to create a future that we can all be excited about!

May 2019

My friend and I built a smart device that helps blind people identify objects in their surroundings.
